UX Process

  1. User Research: To understand common frustrations with online merch shopping, I reviewed popular e-commerce sites and gathered informal feedback from potential users. Insights revealed that users valued fast load times, clear product visuals, and an easy checkout process, but often encountered cluttered layouts, slow pages, and unclear CTAs.
  2. User Flow Mapping: I outlined the key user journeys such as browsing products, viewing product details, adding items to cart, and checking out. The goal was to make each step intuitive and reduce unnecessary friction â€” especially on mobile.
  3. Wireframing: SLow-fidelity wireframes were sketched to explore layout options and content structure. Special focus was placed on the product grid, filters, and cart flow to ensure a smooth and accessible experience from discovery to purchase.
  4. UI Design: Using Figma, I created high-fidelity screens with a clean, bold visual style that aligns with the youthful, trendy nature of merchandise culture. I used generous spacing, consistent components, and legible typography (Inter) to ensure a crisp and modern look across devices.
  5. Prototyping & Interaction: I developed an interactive prototype to test navigation, cart behavior, and the checkout process. I paid special attention to micro-interactions like button feedback, image zoom on hover, and confirmation states.
  6. Usability Testing & Iteration: After initial testing with a small group, I refined the design based on feedback â€” improving mobile responsiveness, spacing in the product grid, and visibility of error/success messages during checkout.
